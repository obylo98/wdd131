Abuja, Nigeria

Nigeria, in West Africa, is the continent's most populous country with over 200 million people and diverse landscapes from coastal plains to mountains. It has over 250 ethnic groups, including the Hausa-Fulani, Yoruba, and Igbo. Nigeria's economy is Africa's largest, driven by oil, gas, agriculture, and services. Since independence from Britain in 1960, it is a federal republic with 36 states and the capital in Abuja. Known for its rich culture, music, dance, and Nollywood film industry, Nigeria faces challenges like political instability and poverty but remains a key African nation.
